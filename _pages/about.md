Hi! My name is Kanav Mittal, and welcome to my corner of the Internet! I will be a Software Engineer at NVIDIA starting August 2026 working on AI for chip design (VLSI). 

I recently graduated with my M.S. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) from UC Berkeley where I worked on [AI (mis)alignment in education](/assets/Kanav%20Mittal%20-%20MS%20Thesis.pdf) with Professor Gireeja Ranade. I was graciously supported by the Siebel Scholarship. Before that, I graduated with my B.S. in EECS from Berkeley as well where I worked with Professor Nilah Ioannidis and Ruchir Rastogi on [machine learning for genomics](/assets/Kanav%20Mittal%20-%20Senior%20Thesis.pdf).

At Berkeley, I also served as a TA for EECS 16A (Designing Information Devices and Systems), CS 61B (Data Structures), and CS 188 (Artificial Intelligence). I am also thankful to Professors Narges Norouzi and Serina Chang for their mentorship on other research projects, and to Professors Gasper Begus and Antje Postema for a wonderful time living at Bowles Hall Residential College.

In my free time, I enjoy hiking or running the beautiful Bay Area trails, cheering on local sports teams, and cooking up new fusion recipes.
